Meet The Enemy: Pishacha

November 29, 2025

Welcome to INTEL BULLETIN — a series diving into the entities and characters that inhabit the world of The Broken Series. These aren't your typical horror monsters. They're drawn from real mythology, real folklore, real nightmares that humanity has been whispering about for millennia. First up: the flesh-eaters.

Every culture has stories about things that feed on the dead. Most people assume they're metaphors—ways to process grief, fear of mortality, warnings to respect burial rites. They're not metaphors. The following intelligence bulletin was compiled from network assets. Read it carefully.

Physical Profile

Humanoid configuration. Height variable: 1.5 to 2.1 meters observed. Emaciated muscular structure with pronounced skeletal definition. Skin coloration ranges from necrotic gray to mottled charcoal depending on feeding cycle. Cranial structure elongated with prominent orbital ridges. Dentition: dual rows of needle-configuration teeth optimized for tearing flesh from bone.

Eyes exhibit thermal luminescence in low-light conditions—red to amber spectrum. Hands terminate in elongated digits with keratinous claws measuring 5-8 centimeters. Spinal curvature suggests quadrupedal capability despite bipedal preference. Odor signature: decay, sulfur, burned meat.

Behavioral Analysis

Primarily nocturnal. Demonstrates strong territorial behavior around cremation sites, battlefields, hospitals, and locations with high mortality density. Hunting pattern: patient stalking followed by rapid assault. Preferred prey: the dying, the grieving, the spiritually weakened.

Documented capacity to induce sleep paralysis in targets prior to feeding. Victim reports consistent: pressure on chest, inability to move, entity observed in peripheral vision before full manifestation. Feeds on flesh but demonstrates particular interest in REDACTED tissue. Will consume carrion but actively hunts living prey when available.

Social structure: typically solitary, but post-breach observations indicate pack behavior emerging in high-density manifestation zones.

Historical Context

Sanskrit texts dating to 1500 BCE document Pishacha activity. Consistent descriptions across Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain sources suggest reliable witness testimony rather than mythological embellishment. Ancient accounts describe them as "flesh-eaters who haunt cremation grounds" — accurate assessment.

Traditional countermeasures include mantras (Maha Mrityunjaya demonstrated 34% efficacy in field tests), Neem leaves (irritant, not lethal), and specific ritual protections. Modern small arms effective for physical disruption but regeneration observed within REDACTED minutes unless countermeasures applied.

Threat Assessment

Individual Pishacha: manageable threat for trained operator with appropriate loadout. Pack configuration: significant danger requiring team response. Primary threat vectors: ambush predation, sleep paralysis incapacitation, psychological degradation of isolated personnel.

Do not engage near active cremation or burial sites—territorial response escalates to swarming behavior. Do not engage while fatigued, grieving, or spiritually compromised—they sense weakness.

Corporeal manifestation means conventional weapons achieve effect. Aim center mass, confirm kills, maintain spacing. They are fast. They are patient. They remember faces.

Engagement Protocols

Refer to Network Operations Manual Section REDACTED for detailed engagement procedures. Summary: maintain team integrity, employ overlapping fields of fire, designate spotter for flanking detection. Spiritual support assets recommended but not required for Class II threats.

If sleep paralysis onset detected: do not panic. Controlled breathing. Focus on extremity movement. They feed on fear before they feed on flesh.
